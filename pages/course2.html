Cyber Security

Course Description

The Cyber Security course is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of securing digital assets, identifying and mitigating cyber threats, and implementing defense mechanisms to protect against various types of cyberattacks. This course covers foundational principles, tools, technologies, and practices used in the field of cybersecurity, preparing students for real-world challenges and career opportunities in this fast-growing domain.

Course Modules

Module 1: Introduction to Cyber Security

Importance of cyber security in today's digital world.

Key concepts: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad).

Threat landscape: Understanding risks, vulnerabilities, and threats.

Cybersecurity frameworks and standards (NIST, ISO 27001, etc.).

Module 2: Types of Cyber Threats and Attacks

Malware (viruses, worms, ransomware, spyware).

Phishing and social engineering.

Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks.

Insider threats and advanced persistent threats (APTs).

Module 3: Cryptography and Data Security

Basics of encryption and decryption.

Symmetric vs. asymmetric encryption.

Hashing algorithms (MD5, SHA).

Secure communication protocols (TLS/SSL).
